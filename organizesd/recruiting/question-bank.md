# Recruit Question Bank (Long Form)

This file preserves the full question bank for application/interview use.

Source: Organize SD “Recruit Application” doc (65+ questions).

## Motivation and fit
1. Why do you want to join Organize SD, and why now?
2. What’s one specific outcome you want from this program by the end of the cohort?
3. What does “grassroots movement building” mean to you in practice?
4. What’s a campaign, issue, or community effort that inspired you—and why?
5. What’s the hardest thing about organizing in South Dakota right now (in your view)?
6. What communities do you feel most accountable to, and how do you show that accountability?
7. What would make this program not a good fit for you?
8. What would you contribute to the cohort as a peer?
9. Which training topics are you most eager to learn? Which are you least excited about? Why?
10. What motivates you more: building systems behind the scenes, or persuading people face-to-face?

## Values, leadership, and accountability
11. Tell us about a time you took responsibility for a mistake. What did you do next?
12. Describe a time you gave someone hard feedback—or received it well.
13. How do you handle conflict on a team when stakes are high?
14. What does “discipline” look like in organizing work?
15. How do you build trust with people who are skeptical of politics?
16. What’s your approach to working with people who don’t fully agree with you?
17. What does leadership look like when you don’t have a title?
18. How do you prevent burnout—for yourself and for your team?
19. What boundaries do you need to do your best work?
20. How do you stay organized when you have multiple responsibilities?

## Organizing experience (formal or informal)
21. What is the most relevant organizing experience you’ve had?
22. Have you ever recruited volunteers? How did you do it?
23. Have you ever trained someone else to do a task?
24. Describe a time you increased participation in something.
25. Have you ever had to “start from zero” to build a group or program? What did you do first?
26. What kind of voter contact have you done (doors, phones, text, relational)? What did you learn?
27. Comfort level talking to strangers—what helps you do it well?
28. Describe a tough organizing conversation (rejection/hostility/misinformation). How did you handle it?
29. Experience working with rural communities/small towns?
30. Experience working in coalition with groups that have different priorities?

## Field skills and program thinking
31. If goal = 500 voter contacts in 7 days, how would you plan the week?
32. Approach to setting daily goals and tracking progress?
33. Explain “follow-up” to a new volunteer in one minute.
34. What makes a good turf plan?
35. What does quality control look like for canvassing/phones?
36. How do you get a volunteer to come back a second time?
37. Training someone nervous/inexperienced—what’s your approach?
38. How do you prioritize when everything feels urgent?
39. Activity vs impact—what’s the difference?
40. Describe a time you used data/tracking to improve results.

## Communication and persuasion
41. Give a 30-second pitch for why someone should vote in Nov 2026.
42. Tailoring message to different audiences without losing authenticity.
43. Response to: “Both parties are the same.”
44. Response to: “My vote doesn’t matter.”
45. Handling misinformation heard at doors/phones.
46. Public speaking / facilitation experience.
47. Example message you wrote that got action (text/email/post/flyer).
48. Strongest channels (in-person/phone/text/social) and why.

## Reliability and professionalism
49. What does “dependable” mean in organizing context?
50. Juggling responsibilities while meeting commitments—example.
51. Current availability (hours/week) during training period.
52. Consistent access to phone + internet?
53. Reliable transportation?
54. What would prevent completion and how can we plan around it?
55. Feedback/coaching preferences.

## Travel and logistics
56. Ability to travel to training location?
57. Travel radius.
58. Barriers to travel.
59. Need travel support (mileage/hotel/childcare stipend)?
60. Comfort with multi-day weekend intensives?

## Culture, cohort dynamics, and learning style
61. Learning environment preference.
62. Experience on teams with people from different backgrounds.
63. Disagreement with friend/family member—how do you navigate?
64. How do you contribute when you’re new?
65. What does respectful, high-performing team culture look like?

## Commitment and next steps
66. If selected, what commitment can you make to completing the full cohort and assignments?
67. Organizing role you’re aiming for after program?
68. Open to partner opportunities after graduation?
69. Communities/regions you’re most interested in organizing with?
70. What would success look like 6 months after finishing?

## Scenario-based (great for interviews)
71. Volunteer repeatedly cancels—what do you do?
72. Two strong volunteers dislike each other—what do you do?
73. Numbers behind with 3 days left—what changes?
74. Someone shares screenshot of voter data—what do you do?
75. Community partner thinks program is “using” their community—how respond?
